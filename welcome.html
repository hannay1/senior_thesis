Investigations in network & application security

Introduction

You are invited to take part in this experiment, which aims to investigate the security of various devices connected to Colorado College’s network. Your participation is necessary as you will be mimicking the actions of a typical user on the network.

Your participation will take approximately half an hour, including the time taken to complete the survey and set up your experience. There are no known risks or discomforts associated with this experiment. Taking part in this study is completely voluntary. You may withdraw your participation at any time, and any associated data will be permanently deleted upon your request. If you choose to be in the study you can withdraw at any time without adversely affecting your relationship with anyone at Colorado College. Your responses to the following survey will be kept strictly confidential, and digital data will be stored in secure files. You will be rewarded with $5 for your time upon the completion of your participation in the experiment.

Furthermore, your actions on this network will not be recorded in a way that can be traced back to you, nor will any sensitive information (such as that found on your various online accounts) will be recorded. By connecting to this network, you have been given an anonymous ID, and all actions will be filed under this ID and this ID only.

Any report of this research that is made available to the public will not include your name or any other individual information by which you could be identified. If you have questions or want a copy or summary of this study’s results, you can contact the researcher at the email address above. If you have any questions about whether you have been treated in an illegal or unethical way, contact the Colorado College Institutional Research Board chair, Amanda Udis-Kessler at 719-227-8177 or audiskessler@coloradocollege.edu. Please feel free to print a copy of this consent page to keep for your records.
